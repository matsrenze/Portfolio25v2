Consuminder – A digital solution to mitigate foodwaste

Visit Project

Overview

The application is designed to support users in making more responsible and more consistent food choices by reducing decision fatigue and providing structured behavioural guidance. Through the provided support the application aims to mitigate food waste resulting from loss of oversight.

Building on extensive behavioural research and iterative strategy development, the concept translates complex cognitive and emotional drivers of food selection into a practical, easy-to-use digital tool.

Across the development process, we analysed user intentions, barriers, motivational triggers, and contextual influences (as shown in the behavioural maps and clustered insights on the research boards). Through this analysis, the app positions itself as a decision-support system: it simplifies daily choices, nudges users toward nutritious options, and removes friction around planning and preparation.

Progress

I contributed across the full design process, assisting in user research, behavioural modelling, and early ideation. I helped analyse user motivations and barriers, translate insights into behavioural frameworks, and generate concept directions grounded in these models. I also supported the creation of initial wireframes to define core interaction flows.

My primary responsibility was developing the second iteration of the prototype, where I refined the structure, improved usability, and integrated the behavioural strategies established during research. This iteration served as a clearer, more cohesive foundation for continued testing and development.

Insights

I analysed user behaviours around planning, shopping, and cooking, and synthesised findings from behavioural models and research sessions. Key insights were clear:

Users often forgot what they had at home, leading to unused ingredients and unnecessary purchases. Decision fatigue caused users to choose convenience foods instead of using what was already in their kitchen.

Emotional factors such as stress or low energy disrupted meal planning, increasing the likelihood of waste.Users found tracking tools overwhelming unless suggestions were simple, timely, and directly actionable.Personalised, context-aware reminders significantly increased the chance that users would use ingredients before they expired.
